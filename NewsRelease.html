John and Susan Tanner to speak at BYU

PROVO, Utah (Oct. 24, 2023) – Former BYU-Hawaii president John S. Tanner and former Young Women General President Susan W. Tanner will be giving a forum address at the Marriott Center on Oct. 31.

Both John and Susan Tanner have deep ties to BYU, having graduated from the University and worked for it, and both have served in leadership for the Church.

John Tanner wrote BYU’s Educational Aims, served as BYU’s Associate Academic Vice President for Undergraduate and International Education from 1992 to 1998, and oversaw changes in BYU’s General Education requirements. A focus in his career has been to synthesize scholarly and religious views, done through writings and his teachings as a professor. John Tanner also wrote the words to “Bless Our Fast We Pray.”

Susan Tanner served as the Young Women General President from 2002 to 2007, after which she wrote “Daughters in My Kingdom,” a book distributed to all adult women in The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. She graduated with a degree in Humanities from BYU, with an emphasis in English and French.

The Tanners often speak about education and believe that education enables individuals to reach their full potential.

“An education that edifies does not destroy innocence but pushes back ignorance,” said John Tanner.

In previous speeches, the Tanners have spoken on the worth of education and how education can prepare individuals to resolve the social issues of today. Listen live at the Marriott Center, on BYUtv, or BYU Radio; it will be available to review later at www.speeches.byu.edu.


About BYU
Brigham Young University is a private university owned by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints dedicated to “assisting individuals in their quest for perfection and eternal life.” 34,390 students from 105 students study at BYU, which is ranked number 11 for Best Value School by the U.S. News. Weekly campus devotionals are meant to “enlighten, uplift, instruct, and give hope to listeners.” For more information about forums and devotionals, see www.speeches.byu.edu.
