Subject: How women make UN peace agreements last longer

Margaret,

I recently read your piece on the UN Security Council approving international support of Haiti’s police. It helped me see how international support is a step towards restoring democracy and independence in Haiti – something I’ve learned a bit about from a colleague who lived there.

I can tell you’re passionate about the UN’s impact, and thought you might be interested in a new study that found the UN Security Council is not as effective in creating peace treaties as they could be. The study found that Resolution 1325, which requires women be adequately represented on peacekeeping committees, is being ignored. There is substantial research showing women are essential to creating long-lasting peace treaties – treaties with women involvement are 35% more likely to last 15 years or more.

If you’re interested, I can forward you a copy of the study or set up an interview with the lead professor.

Thanks,
Emily
